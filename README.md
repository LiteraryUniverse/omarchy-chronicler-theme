# Chronicler

A theme for those who record the world as it unfolds — the field naturalist's notebook, the botanical illustrator's
desk, the illuminator's workshop at the height of the Art Nouveau movement. Surfaces bloom with the organic warmth of
pressed flowers and hand-lettered captions: deep forest greens, burnished copper, and the rich violet-brown of iron
gall ink. The Literary Universe magenta appears as a rare pigment — a touch of carmine in the margin.

Where Manuscript evokes the finished page, Chronicler evokes the act of making it: the open sketchbook, the ink still
wet, the vine border still being drawn.

## Color Philosophy

- **Background**: Deep forest parchment — aged field notebook
- **Foreground**: Iron gall ink — the naturalist's hand
- **Primary accent**: LU magenta (`#A12577`) — carmine pigment, the rare flourish
- **Secondary accent**: Forest green (`#4A7C59`) — botanical illustration, vine borders
- **Tertiary accent**: Burnished copper (`#B87333`) — the illuminator's metallic ink
- **Quaternary accent**: Violet-brown (`#6B4E71`) — the shadow in the vine
- **Error / warning**: Ochre red — a correction in the margin

## Art Direction

Art Nouveau at its most organic: sinuous vine borders, asymmetric flourishes, the natural world pressed into geometry.
Rounded corners, flowing animations, deep shadows that suggest depth on the page.
